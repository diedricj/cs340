All About Clay!

Clay Origin Uses Color Transparency
Porcelain Porcelain originated from China, and is a combination of clay and flint, meant to have small particles and a very small particle size Porcelain is used to create very strong pieces that can be either painted or glazed Porcelain is very popular for its white color in appearance that can be achieved with no glaze Porcelain is also very popular for its transparency. This can be achieved when porcelain is thrown with very thin walls, and lets light shine through the small particle size.
Rocky Road Rocky Road is a general name for mixed clay. It can come from a variety of places. It usually has a larger particle size and is very industrial. Rocky Road is relatively cheap and can be used for learning. It is a utility clay, while it is not excellent in any category, it can be used for almost anything. Rocky Road is typically a light gray color, and is usually applied a glazing to add color after firing. Rocky Road is not transparent.
White Salmon White Salmon is a mix of normal clay that is white in color. It has thin clay particles, and is meant to be a cheaper utility clay that resembles Porcelain. White Salmon is easier to throw with than porcelain clay, because it tends to absorb less water and maintain structure, but can still resemble porcelain in many ways. White Salmon has a white-ish pink color, that mimics that of Porcelain's whiteness. White Salmon and very thin spots in the clay can be transparent, but usually this is not achievable.
